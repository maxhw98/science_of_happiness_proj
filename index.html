Sleep & Nutrition:

The mind and body are not separate! Your physical health is an important part of your mental health as well. Many students have been told that maintaining a proper sleep schedule and nutrition are essential to their health and performance in school. However, school itself can cause usual sleep and eating cycles to become chaotic and irregular, which contributes to a negative downhill spiral. Below are some resources for restoring and maintaining one’s sleep and eating cycles. AMSA Healthy Eating for Students Guide Meditation: Meditation is a well known coping mechanism for stress and anxiety that has been used for hundreds of years. Though the popular notion of mediation is limited to closing your eyes and sitting very still, there are actually many different forms of meditation that one can use. An example of a more active meditation technique is tensing up and releasing different parts of the body, starting from the head and moving down to the feet. A grounding meditation technique can even be as simple as going for a walk while keeping a certain color in mind and noting each time it appears. Cognitive Behavioral Therapy Exercises: Do you find yourself going through the same patterns of negative thoughts and behaviors over and over again? One bad grade might not ruin your semester, but a pattern of catastrophizing will only serve to increase your stress. Cognitive behavioral therapy (CBT) is one way psychologists tackle negative thought patterns. It’s based on the CBT model of emotion, where emotions are broken down into thoughts, feelings, and behaviors. For example, one might have a thought that studying for the exam is pointless because they haven’t covered enough of the material beforehand. This leads to a feeling of hopelessness that can lead to a behavior of not studying at all that day, which then can lead to the thought, “Oh no, I have so much left to study!” Then comes the feeling of hopelessness, and the cycle continues. The theory is that if you can make a change in one of these three parts of the cycle, the rest will change as well. These exercises are not a replacement for a mental health professional, however, they can be hugely helpful in understanding the causes of your stress and how to respond to them. https://cogbtherapy.com/introduction-to-cbt - Free CBT Workbook Organizational Techniques and Tools: Organizing your work is one step that can greatly reduce your stress when dealing with large projects or assignments.
